Explanations of what happened¶

My Policy function iteration does not converge but it works. After looking at the debug information, I realize that policy functions are looping over iterations. This is on one iteration policy function is assigned value X, on the next Y, then again X, then again Y and so on and on and on. For $\gamma=5$ the pattern is more complex, with iterations repeating sequence of 5 values.

This is not surprising due to the nature of interpolation. If the two values are almost equidistant to the actual best policy, then iterations can enter a loop. Probably, the best strategy is to terminate the algorithm and take average of the 2 policy rules.

Turning to evaluating my results¶

The variance of simulations with 10000 values vary too much and the results for different values of $\gamma$ are varying. I increased number of simulations to 100000 times, but the problem is not completely solved. The standard deviations and means computed for $\gamma=1$ and $\gamma=2$ are very similar. SD and Mean for $\gamma=5$ are smaller. This is consistent with agents being more risk-averse and willing to trade some mean consumption for decrease in standard deviation.

I tried to code EGM, however the algorithm does not converge but rather explodes to infinite consumption. After doing a bit of debuggins, I realized, that for some reason, my implementation of EGM completely ignores feasibility. Many $a^*$ and $a^*_0$ are simply negative, which is not good.
I tried different solutions, i.e. by setiing $a^*$ to zero if it is negative but it does not seem to work. But what I can definitely say, that if EGM worked it would be orders of magniture faster than PFI. However, there is speed stability trade-off in this case.
